An Historical Perspective

The term AI was coined by John McCarthy in 1955 when setting up a group to investigate “thinking machines” at Dartmouth College.  Defined as “the science and engineering of making intelligent machines”; its progression was slow during the 1960s and 1970s due to the limited speed and capacity of computers. 

Optional Reading 

This short blog post The History of Artificial Intelligence by Rockwell Anyoha will take you through the early days of AI. You might also like to explore this other short article which explores the legacy of John McCarthy and other seminal scientists in this area like Alan Turing - Artificial Solutions (2020) 

It was not until the fields of machine learning and neural networks reached maturity in the 1990s that investment and interest in AI gained momentum. The development of much more powerful, and distributed, computer systems, together with the collection of large quantities of data, digital imagery and the availability of a range of sensors, enabled AI to be applied in a range of domains, including real-time processing.
