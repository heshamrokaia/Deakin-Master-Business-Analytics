What is superintelligence

Superintelligence refers to the concept of creating machines or artificial agents with cognitive abilities that surpass those of humans.

What is Superintelligence - text alternative

Although Artificial Intelligence can assist us in various tasks and is already doing so, it is important to note that it is distinct from natural intelligence and constitutes a completely different phenomenon than human intelligence. Floridi (2016) asserted that although 'true AI' is not logically impossible to achieve, the likelihood of it occurring is highly unlikely. Dignum (2019 p. 117) shared the same view by questioning: 

I often run marathons. I don't doubt that it is possible to build a `running robot' but will it ever experience, and enjoy, what it means to run a marathon, to push through the pain and enjoy it?

The field is divided on whether it is feasible to construct super-intelligent machines. Nevertheless, instead of concentrating on the possibility of creating such machines, it is more pertinent to examine our thoughts about it.

The development of superintelligence raises various philosophical, ethical, and practical concerns. Some experts argue that it could lead to a new era of understanding the universe and solving some of humanity's greatest challenges, while others warn of catastrophic consequences, including machines developing motivations or goals that conflict with our own, leading to unintended consequences.

The idea of super intelligence is related to the pursuit of creating machines that can perform any task that a human being is capable of, and/or also surpass human intelligence, which is sometimes referred to as "true AI”. Also, from an ethical and responsibility perspective, we should explore if we should develop such machines and what the implications of success would be.
