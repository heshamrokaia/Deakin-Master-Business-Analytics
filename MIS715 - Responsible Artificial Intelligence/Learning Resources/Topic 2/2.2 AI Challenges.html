AI Challenges - What people think about AI

A survey by KPMG (2021) of 6,054 citizens from United States, Canada, Germany, the United Kingdom and Australia found that for AI to be trusted and ultimately accepted, the following questions need to be answered:

  • Can current regulations and laws make AI safe to use?
  • How will AI impact jobs?
  • Can the use and operation of AI be understood?
  • What impacts will AI have on society? 

They suggest a model that lays out a pathway to building trust and acceptance of AI. The path model shows the impact that central drivers have on trust and acceptance of AI.

The model is read from left to right and starts by showing the drivers that impact on trust. The B values indicate the relative importance in driving trust and acceptance, the larger the value the stronger the impact it has. 

Drag the slider to labelled points to explore the model.

Optional Reading 

In this short article for Today’s General Counsel,  AI Lacks Empathy, Needs Humans in the Middle , they consider if AI is ready to assume human qualities that emphasize empathy, ethics, and morality.

While there are opportunities for many tasks to be performed by AI systems, they are not ready to make decisions without human supervision, apart from perhaps some low risk, low probability situations such as should I put the washing out today. The quintessential example is the "trolley problem" (Greene, 2016) whose origins lie in the study of moral philosophy.
