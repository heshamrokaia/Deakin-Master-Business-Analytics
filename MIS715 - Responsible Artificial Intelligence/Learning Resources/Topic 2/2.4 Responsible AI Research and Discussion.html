Responsible AI Research and Discussion

AI technologies are conceived through research and innovation and as such are the starting points for responsible AI. To do this, the broader context of AI's implementation needs to be studied. Mills and Lim (2020) suggested the five following ways to join the RAI discussion

floatleft

Customer dialogs

Add Responsible AI to your existing customer and community dialogs

floatleft

Diversity matters

Engage with diverse stakeholders and proactively address their concerns

floatleft

Be deliberate

Give your program time to mature instead of trying to go from zero to press release

floatleft

Set the right tone

Set a tone of openness and transparency to build a culture of sharing

Renieris et al’. (2022) extracted an AI’s expert’s opinion:

AI systems can fail or succeed, and any successful AI system must be ethically responsible. The idea that engineering and ethics can be separated in certain ways should be rejected. Saying, ‘This system is itself successful but only ethically fails,’ is a contradiction in terms. That’s similar to saying, ‘He is a great person but just harms others.’ If an AI system is really successful, it must be ethically mature. Hence, if it is not ethically mature, it is not a successful AI.
