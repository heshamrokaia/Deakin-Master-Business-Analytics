Governance strategy to mitigate AI risks

In this section, we will investigate responsibility at the strategic level where organisations need to consider AI risk management while developing their strategic plan for IT governance. 

There are several AI governance strategies proposed by different organisations. The framework we will examine below, applies a comprehensive approach, is well-written and leverages extensive legal knowledge to provide practical and legally compliant guidance for organisations that are looking to adopt responsible and ethical AI practices.

To maximize the benefits that AI can bring to organisations, AI risks and damages need to be monitored and managed. Dentons, one of the world’s largest law firms suggested the following strategy for organisations focusing on mitigating AI risks (Lucas, 2021).

floatleft

AI Taskforce and Governance Board

Establish an AI taskforce and governance board for developing ethical program/framework and overseeing AI strategy and implementation.

floatleft

Governance principles

High-level principles to govern the organisation’s approach to AI ethics. The principles should be relevant to the technology that the organisation uses and specific to business and industry that the organisation operates in. These principles also must be institutionalised in different levels of the business, integrated into leadership communications, conduct guidelines, training courses and rewards systems. Organisation’s staff should be encouraged and empowered to raise any ethical concerns they encounter.

floatleft

Risk assessment

Conduct a comprehensive AI ethics risk assessment to identify where risks are most likely to occur and pre-empt ethical issues. Embed changes into organisation’s governance processes and introduce human oversight to effectively clarify the issue of accountability for AI decisions. Create an AI governance plan that should include short and medium-term goals and guidance to achieve the goals, and long-term direction into AI governance plan. It is important to update the plan regularly with appropriately well-trained and qualified individuals holding ownership of the plan.

floatleft

Monitor AI Impacts 

Regularly monitor the impact of AI as ethical issues may arise unexpectedly. While assessing potential risks of AI before implementing the system is essential, frequently monitoring the impact of the system once it is in use is critical as it will enable organisations to respond quickly to identify the issues and alleviate the damage.

floatleft

Dataset management 

Ensure data sets used for training AI are complete, current, and representative to reduce the potential for bias and discrimination issues. Also, data protection and privacy procedures must be robust and transparent.

floatleft

Like-minded partners 

Check with AI providers and future business partners to make sure they share similar ethical standard as your organisation. 

Source: Lucas, A. (2021). Key challenges of artificial intelligence: AI ethics and governance. Dentons.
