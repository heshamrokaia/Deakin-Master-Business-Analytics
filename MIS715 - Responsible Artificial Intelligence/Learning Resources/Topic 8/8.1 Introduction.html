Human Rights and Dignity and their Relation to AI

Artificial intelligence (AI) has the potential to enhance your life, liberty and security.  But as youâ€™ve seen throughout this unit, it can also adversely impact them in various ways.

The human rights concerns relating to life, liberty, and security, as well as the freedom of speech, are of utmost significance. It is crucial to carefully evaluate the potential hazards of AI utilisation in contrast to the hazards of not utilising AI, as well as other forms of innovative technologies (Leslie, 2019).

As you watch this video, think about freedom of expression and access to information as human rights. 

How have the enjoyment of these human rights changed since social media and AI became more prevalent? Can the enjoyment of these human rights be separated from our media of communications? How are other human rights, say, the right to vote and the right to education, similarly dependent on digital technologies?

In this topic you will consider a range of ethical questions and potential solutions to address AI human rights issues related to life, liberty and security of an individual. Some of the proposed solutions include enhancing liability frameworks, establishing quality management systems and promoting adversarial robustness. It is imperative that AI developers, deployers and users uphold the sanctity of human life and prioritise this principle throughout the design, development and use of their products and/or services. It is of utmost importance that AI systems are not programmed to cause harm or injury to humans. 

To illustrate how AI can affect human life, liberty and security we will use three examples from the areas of transportation (self-driving cars), home environment (smart security systems) and healthcare services (adversarial attacks).
