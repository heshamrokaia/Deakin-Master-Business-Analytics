Examples of AI Adversely Affecting the Right to Life, Liberty and Security of Persons

Let’s look at four different examples and analyse the situations where AI systems have affected human life, liberty and security. The first two are from a transportation context (self-driving cars), the third relates to the home environment (smart home security), and the last one is from a healthcare service setting (medical diagnosis).

floatleft

Example 1: Fatal Crash Involving a Self-driving Car

The first recorded incident of a self-driving car to be involved in a fatal crash occurred in May 2016 when a Tesla car collided with a tractor-trailer resulting in the death of the 42-year-old passenger/driver. The tractor driver was not injured. Tesla’s explanation of the crash was that the car’s sensor system was unable to differentiate a large white 18-wheel truck and trailer crossing the highway due to the bright spring sky (Levin and Woolf 2016). An examination by the Florida Highway Patrol concluded that the Tesla driver had been negligent and failed to take evasive action while the tractor driver had failed, during a left turn, to give right of way, according to the report (Golson 2017).

floatleft

Example 2: Self-driving car incident in Melbourne, Australia

A report from Channel 9 News warns drivers who use the Autopilot feature in self-driving cars. A 23-year-old driver was charged with dangerous driving causing serious injury and other charges following a hit and run incident involving Tesla Model 3 car.  The driver’s Tesla was on autopilot when the car failed to stop when a person was crossing the road to board a tram around 6:30am.  

Driving experts have expressed concerns about the dangers of self-driving cars, claiming that the Autopilot function is an assistance tool, and not to replace the driver. This was the first incident that the Victoria Police’s Major Collision Investigators had attended involving an autonomous driving capability feature.

floatleft

Example 3: Smart Home Hubs Security Vulnerabilities

A smart home hub is a centralised control system for home automation systems, including heating, blinds, lights and internet-connected electronic devices. A user is able to remotely interact with the hub using a smartphone allowing them to arrive at home with the networked gas fire burning and supper ready in the networked oven. However, if there are security weaknesses, external entities may also be able access these smart home hubs, as was the case for three companies operating across Europe, not just solely the users (Fránik and ˇCermák 2020).

Example 4: Adversarial Attacks in Medical Diagnosis

The use of medical images is crucial in medical diagnosis, particularly in radiology. Adversarial attacks on medical image analysis systems have become a significant problem, and can endanger lives (Bortsova et al., 2021). This issue is relevant whether the AI system or radiologists are responsible for the medical diagnosis.  Zhou et al. conducted an experiment to test the vulnerability of medical image analysis systems to adversarial attacks. They used a generative adversarial network (GAN) model to intentionally modify radiology images that are typically used to identify breast cancer (Zhou et al., 2021). The altered images were then evaluated by an AI model and by radiologists. The results showed that the adversarial samples were able to deceive the AI-CAD model, causing it to produce an incorrect diagnosis in 69.1% of the cases that were initially correctly classified by the AI-CAD.  The findings of the experiment suggest that adversarial attacks on medical image analysis systems can pose a significant threat and lead to serious consequences. This highlights the need for improved security measures to prevent such attacks.
