Preventing AI Human rights concerns

Preventing AI human rights concerns involves taking proactive steps to ensure that AI technologies are developed, deployed and used in a manner that respects and upholds human rights. It requires a proactive, collaborative, and ongoing effort. Given the potential issues and concerns, it is important to put considerable effort into preventing AI human rights issues arising around life, liberty and security of persons, for which the ethical frameworks, and governance for responsible AI that you learnt in weeks 6 and 7 will be particularly helpful.

The fundamental human right is the right to life, which serves as the foundation for all other rights. The right to life is interconnected with other human rights, such as privacy and dignity.

In the context of AI, it requires developers, deployers and users to respect the value and sanctity of human life and therefore it is essential that this principle is incorporated into each step of the design, development and usage of products and/or services. And that it should be prohibited to program AI systems to kill or injure humans.

In situations where there is a significant possibility of harm, even if unintentional, appropriate measures are needed to ensure that their products and services do not cause harm to people. These steps could include:   

  • providing adequate training, 
  • using safety-based design, 
  • providing transparency and accountability in the development and deployment of AI,
  • conducting rigorous testing and evaluation of AI systems before they are deployed in the real-world setting,
  • improving accident investigation and reporting,
  • encouraging collaboration and engagement between stakeholders to identify and mitigate potential harms.

Although AI has exceeded human expectations, it is crucial that AI supports human life and not undermine it. Failure to acknowledge the harms and adverse impacts of AI can lead to problematic actions that are resistant to change. Sensitivity to these issues requires an understanding of the needs and taking useful actions to fulfil that need. Additionally, it is essential to remember that AI can influence, change and damage human life in various ways, in ways that may currently be unknown.

These dimensions should be used as guiding values to the specific social and ethical problems raised by the potential misuse, abuse, poor design, or harmful unintended consequences of AI systems.
