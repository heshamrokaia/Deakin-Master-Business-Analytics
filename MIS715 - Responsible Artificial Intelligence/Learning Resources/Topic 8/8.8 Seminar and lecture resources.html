Seminar and lecture resources

Preparation for the seminar: 

  • Read the Topic 9 sections and complete activities.
  • Take notes to help you learn and organise information
  • Read the following articles.
    • Snyder (2019) What are the impacts of artificial intelligence on human rights, and how can adverse impacts be prevented or mitigated? The AI & Human Rights Symposium sought answers. Stanford University. The link to the article can be found here
    • Zardiashvili L, Fosch-Villaronga E (2020) “Oh, dignity too?” said the robot: human dignity as the basis for the governance of robotics. Minds Mach 30:121–143. https://doi.org/10.1007/s11023-019-09514-6  Access via the library
    • Caine M (2020) This is how AI could feed the world’s hungry while sustaining the planet. World Economic Forum, 24 September. https://www.weforum.org/agenda/2020/09/this-is-how-ai-could-feed-the-world-s-hungry-while-sustaining-the-planet/
  • Note down any questions to bring up during the class

In Seminar tasks

Task 1. Together with your peers, explain how AI can potentially promote and also violate human rights and human dignity? Use examples in the readings above to justify your answers.

Task 2. Identify and discuss in detail the approach that Zardiashvily and Fosch-Villaronga (2020) recommend using to address the negative impacts of AI on human dignity

Task 3. Each group will present their answers to the class (or add your answers to the discussion forum)
