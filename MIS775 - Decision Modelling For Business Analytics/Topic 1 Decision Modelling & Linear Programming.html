Introduction

  • Decision Modelling is a field of study that uses computers, statistics, and mathematics to solve business problems
  • Decision Modelling is a scientific approach to managerial decision making. It provides a conceptual framework for thinking systematically through hard decisions
  • The body of knowledge involving quantitative approaches to decision making is referred to as: Management Science or Operations Research or Operational Research (OR) or Decision Science
  • The OR Society defines operational research and its applications in this video:

    

  • Professional societies:
    • INFORMS SOCIETY: The Institute for Operations Research and the Management Sciences (INFORMS) is the largest society in the world for professionals in the field of operations research (O.R.), management science, and analytics

    • The Australian Society for Operations Research (ASOR): The OR society in Australia is also active in development of the science of Operations Research and its applications

    • ASOR offers free membership for students
  • Applications: This video gives examples of OR applications as an advance analytic tools:

    

  • Types of decision models: There are 2 types of DECISION MODELS - Deterministic models and Stochastic models
  • Linear Programming (LP) is a Deterministic model. In LP based decision models, we assume that all the relevant input and parameters are known with certainty i.e. LP based decision modelling techniques are classified as deterministic models.
  • LP involves creating and solving optimisation problems with linear objective functions and linear constraints.
  • The development of all LP models involves : mathematical formulation, solution and interpretation.

    

Learning Objectives/Outcomes

  • Recognise the two types of decision models
  • Understand the steps in the decision modelling process
  • Understand the properties or assumptions of Linear programming
  • Understand the range of possible optimisation outcomes
  • Understand the steps involved in developing an LP decision model
