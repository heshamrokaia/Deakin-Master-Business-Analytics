Introduction

  • NLP model is similar to an LP problem as it is composed of an objective function and constraints
  • LP models assume that the objective function and all of the constraints are linear. Yet, in many real-world business problems, the objective function or a constraint may be nonlinear
  • An NLP model differs from an LP model in that it includes at least one non-linear function, which could be the objective function or a constraint
  • In practice, NLP models are difficult to solve and should be used with caution
  • This topic focuses on formulating NLP models and the challenges involved in solving NLP business problems.

Learning Objectives/Outcomes

  • Understand the differences between LP and NLP models
  • Examine the reasons why NLP models are difficult to solve
  • Understand the process of formulating, implementing and solving NLP models in a spreadsheet
  • Understand how to set-up NLP problems on a spreadsheet and solve them using Excel's Solver routine.
