Introduction

In many situations, a company produces products at locations called origins and ships these products to customer locations called destinations which can be represented by networks.
Supply chain models are network models which are designed to satisfy customer demand for a product at minimum cost. Those that control the supply chain must make decisions such as where to produce a product, how much should be produced, and where it should be sent.
A number of business problems can be represented graphically as networks, such as:
  • Transportation Model
  • Assignment Model
  • Transshipment Model
  • Maximal Flow Model
  • Shortest Path Model.

Learning Objectives/Outcomes

  • Understand why applying optimisation techniques to supply chain problems is useful
  • Understand how to structure special LP network flow models
  • Become familiar with the basic features of transportation, transshipment, assignment, maximal flow, and shortest path models
  • Be able to set-up and solve network models using Solver.
