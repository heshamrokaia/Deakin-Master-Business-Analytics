Introduction

  • Most business outcomes are affected by random variables that introduce uncertainty and hence risk
  • Although the process of capturing a business problem as a mathematical model requires the modeller to make assumptions and omit some details of the real scenario, replacing random variability with a fixed value will result in a model that doesnâ€™t capture risk
  • Using past data, it is possible to capture the range and likelihood of a random variable with a probability distribution
  • This topic focuses on building stochastic decision models in spreadsheets, where randomness is captured, allowing the decision maker to consider the range and likelihood of possible outcomes.
  

Learning Objectives/Outcomes

  • To understand the purpose of simulation
  • Know the steps required in undertaking a simulation
  • Have a thorough understanding of the features of the more commonly used theoretical probability distributions
  • Be able to perform simulation in Excel.
