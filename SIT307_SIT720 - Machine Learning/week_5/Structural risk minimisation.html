Structural risk minimisation

So based on Occam’s razor and its simplistic principle, we define another risk value which is called Structural Risk.

Structural risk minimisation seeks to prevent over-fitting by incorporating a penalty on the model complexity that prefers simpler functions over more complex ones. So the general idea is to minimise both Structural Riskand Empirical Risk which we introduced before:

Rstr(h)=Remp(h)+λC(h){"version":"1.1","math":"R_{str}(h) = R_{emp}(h) + \lambda C(h)"}

Where C(h){"version":"1.1","math":"\(C(h)\)"} is the complexity of hypothesis function h{"version":"1.1","math":"\(h\)"} and λ{"version":"1.1","math":"\(\lambda\)"} is a penalty parameter.

So a comprehensive definition of risk is made of a loss function for over y^i{"version":"1.1","math":"\(\hat{y}_i\)"} and yi{"version":"1.1","math":"\(y_i\)"} for all training points, in addition to the complexity of the proposed model as a penalty.
