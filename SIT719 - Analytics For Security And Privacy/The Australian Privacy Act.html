What regulations are there for the privacy of personal information?

The Australian Privacy Act

The Privacy Act 1988 is an Australian law that deals with the privacy of personal information.

The Act was updated in 2014 to reflect changes in perceptions and views of technology.

Watch the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) video for an overview of the Australian Privacy Act and what is meant by private information.

This is an additional video, hosted on YouTube.

The Privacy Act is based upon Australian Privacy Principles (APPs), outlined in this guidelines video. There are 13 principles including security provisions, for example:

  • Australian Privacy Principle 11 – security of personal information.

This is an additional video, hosted on YouTube.

11.1: If an APP entity holds personal information, the entity must take such steps as are reasonable in the circumstances to protect the information: 1) from misuse, interference and loss and 2) from unauthorised access, modification or disclosure.
(OAIC 2018)

These principles also relate to analytical data and the protection of that data.

The Australian Privacy Act is the only legal requirement for organisations to protect their analytical data and something that very Australian organisation should be made aware of.
